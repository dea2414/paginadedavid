ISU General Computer Science Requirements

The B.S. in Computer Science (General Sequence) requires a minimum of 83 credit hours, including:

  • Core Courses (24 cr): COM 223, ENG 249, IT 168, 179, 180, 191, 214, 225, 261, 279
  • Advanced IT (30 cr): IT 326, 327, 328, 378, 383, 386 and 6 cr from IT 398
  • Mathematics (9 cr): MAT 145, 146, 260
  • Electives (9 cr):
    • IT 276 – Data Communications (3 cr): Hardware and software used in data communications and networking, covering network types, architectures, protocols and standards, and local area and packet networks. Real‑life: equips you to design & troubleshoot corporate and campus networks, optimize bandwidth, and ensure reliable Internet and cloud connectivity. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
    • IT 355 – Secure Software Development (3 cr): Ensuring software is secure through architecture and development techniques. Real‑life: teaches you to build web & mobile applications resistant to common vulnerabilities, protecting user data in banking, healthcare, and e‑commerce. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
    • IT 388 – Parallel Processing (3 cr): Study of parallel processors and their software environments. Students write programs for several parallel computers. Real‑life: underpins high‑performance computing tasks such as scientific simulations, big‑data analytics, and machine learning workloads. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Lab Science (8 cr): Any 2 courses from BSC 196, 197; CHE 140,  141; PHY 110, 111
